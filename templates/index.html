About the Model

A short, honest explanation of ResNet.

What is ResNet?

ResNet, short for Residual Network, is a deep convolutional architecture introduced to make very deep neural networks trainable. Instead of stacking layers blindly, it organizes them into residual blocks connected by shortcuts, allowing the network to grow to 50, 101, or even 152 layers without losing the ability to learn.

Why Residual Connections?

Each block learns a residual — the difference between its input and the desired output — and adds it back through a skip connection. This keeps gradients flowing during training, prevents accuracy from degrading as depth increases, and makes optimization far more stable.

Why Used for Classification?

Depth lets a network build hierarchical features — edges, then textures, then whole objects. ResNet reaches that depth without breaking down, which is why it became a standard backbone for image classification and a foundation for countless vision systems since.
